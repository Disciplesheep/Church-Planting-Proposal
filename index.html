Important Note

The growth projections, budget requirements, and timeline for church planting outlined in this proposal are estimates based on prayerful planning and best practices. However, we acknowledge that:

  • • God's timing is sovereign: The church may be established earlier or later than projected, as the Lord wills.
  • • Growth varies: Actual growth and multiplication rates depend on spiritual receptivity, local conditions, and God's blessing.
  • • Budget flexibility: Financial needs may adjust based on ministry progress and local economic conditions.
  • • Missionary's commitment matters: The dedication, consistency, faithfulness, and spiritual maturity of the missionary directly impact the mission's progress and effectiveness.
  • • Faithfulness over speed: Our primary commitment is to faithful discipleship and quality multiplication, not merely hitting numerical targets.

How Multiplication Works

Generation 1 → 2

I invest in 8-12 faithful leaders through weekly one-on-one discipleship, leadership training, and equipping.

  • • Weekly 1-on-1 meetings
  • • Monthly leadership training
  • • Tools and resources provided
  • • Spiritual accountability

Generation 2 → 3

Each leader disciples 2-4 people (20-36 total), creating multiplication through personal relationships.

  • • Regular 1-on-1 discipleship
  • • Lead small groups (4-6 people)
  • • Practice ministry skills
  • • Teach what they learned

Generation 3 → 4

Disciples reach friends and contacts naturally, sharing faith and inviting to events and gatherings.

  • • Building friendships with seekers
  • • Natural faith conversations
  • • Inviting to gatherings
  • • Sharing personal testimony

Generation 4 → 3

New believers immediately enter discipleship relationships, becoming Generation 3 disciples.

  • • Clear gospel presentation
  • • Immediate discipleship connection
  • • Small group integration
  • • Pathway to becoming leaders

Cross-Pollination Strategy

Professional mentors (all career stages) adopt student leaders. Students bring energy to workplace ministry. This creates a seamless campus-to-workplace pipeline with unified vision.

Cross-Mentoring

Every student leader has a professional mentor. Professionals speak into career and life decisions.

Shared Events

Quarterly combined gatherings, joint service projects, united prayer meetings.

Graduate Transition

Graduating students automatically plugged into workplace ministry. No gap or loss.

Keys to Multiplication Success

  • • Start with Presence: First 2 months just showing up, being available
  • • Look for "People of Peace": Those who welcome you and open networks
  • • Focus on the Few: Invest deeply in 2-4 initially
  • • Go Where People Are: Cafeterias, libraries, food courts
  • • Use Meals & Coffee: Natural, low-pressure contexts
  • • Be Patient: Relationships take time, rushing kills authenticity
  • • Model Everything: Show what disciples should do
  • • Release Early: Let Gen 2 practice before "ready"
  • • Celebrate Small Wins: First study, first conversion, first leader
  • • Stay Dependent on Prayer: Zero requires supernatural breakthrough
